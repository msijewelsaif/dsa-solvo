Stack Operations

A stack is a linear data structure that follows the Last-In-First-Out (LIFO) principle. In a stack, elements are added and removed from the top. The most recently added item is the first to be removed. Stacks are commonly used in various algorithms and applications, such as function call management, expression evaluation, and backtracking. The two primary operations on a stack are "push" to add an element and "pop" to remove the top element.
